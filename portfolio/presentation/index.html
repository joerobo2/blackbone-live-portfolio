02 · Thesis

Applied data science becomes valuable when it stabilizes decisions.

This portfolio is evidence of a method: structure the problem, clarify the state, preserve evidence, and support human action.

Core shift

From isolated reports toward systems that make work reviewable, repeatable, and trusted.

03 · System 1

Financial control.

A fragmented reconciliation challenge became a more explainable control workflow through data cleaning, comparison logic, trend reasoning, and audit-ready communication.

Problem

Financial and operational records did not naturally reconcile into one clean decision surface.

Method

Classify variance, preserve traceability, and make review logic repeatable.

View evidence packet
04 · System 2

Chemical compliance.

A high-consequence reporting process required governance, traceability, review states, and documentation that could support accountable sign-off.

Problem

Compliance information needed to be reviewed, trusted, and reconstructed later.

Method

Build review-state control, sign-off logic, and traceable evidence into the workflow.

View evidence packet
05 · System 3

Inventory control.

Expiration-sensitive inventory became a prioritization surface using FEFO logic, exception review, snapshot evidence, and operational adoption design.

Problem

Visibility alone did not tell users what needed attention first.

Method

Use priority bands, exception detection, filters, and snapshots to support action.

View evidence packet
06 · Cross-system pattern

The same method appeared across all three systems.

Context Structure Modularity Evidence Iteration

The case studies operate in different domains, but they share one deeper method: turn ambiguous operational work into structured, reviewable, decision-support systems.

07 · Reflection

Coursework gave the work language. Work gave the coursework consequence.

The result is a practical method for turning messy operations into shared structure: a way to make information more trustworthy, work more understandable, and decisions more grounded in evidence.

School gave me

Requirements thinking, governance, data modeling, documentation, and evidence-based reasoning.

Work tested

Those ideas against messy data, stakeholder constraints, operational risk, and adoption friction.

08 · Close

From analysis to control, trust, and workflow design.

This portfolio is both an academic artifact and professional evidence of a method.

Final takeaway

I build systems that make information more trustworthy, work more understandable, and decisions more grounded in evidence.
