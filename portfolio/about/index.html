The Perspective Behind the Systems

Doha, Qatar · 2009

Third Culture Kid

Living abroad gave me context clues, adaptability, communication skills, and a broader way of seeing the world. It taught me to see myself as a citizen of the world and to pay attention to how people interpret the same situation from different places.

2009–2024

Air Force Service

After high school, I joined the U.S. Air Force and served from November 2009 to November 2024. Across nearly 15 years of service, including more than a dozen deployments, hundreds of combat missions, and thousands of combat flight hours, I built a foundation in intelligence analysis, operational coordination, training, leadership, and systems integration.

Leadership · training · accountability

Leadership Under Pressure

Service taught me to lead with responsibility, trust, preparation, and care for the people around me. One lesson that stayed with me was to leave things better than when you got there. That belief now shapes how I document work, train others, improve processes, and build tools people can rely on.

Perspective · complexity · decision environments

Clarity in Complexity

Operational work taught me how to switch lenses. I learned to zoom in on the detail in front of me, zoom out to see the larger system, and understand what mattered most in the moment. That skill has stayed with me beyond work, shaping how I read situations, recognize patterns, and move through complexity with clarity.
