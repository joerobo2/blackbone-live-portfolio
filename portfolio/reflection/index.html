Reflection

Coursework and production work became one method.

The degree did not sit separate from the work. The work became the testing ground for the degree, and the degree gave language, structure, and discipline to the work.

What school gave me

  • Requirements thinking
  • Data modeling
  • Analytic communication
  • Governance and documentation
  • Evidence-based reasoning

What production work tested

  • Messy data conditions
  • Stakeholder constraints
  • Adoption friction
  • Operational risk
  • The need for repeatable systems

What changed

The central lesson is that data science becomes valuable when it turns uncertainty into shared structure. School gave me language for modeling, governance, documentation, and evidence-based reasoning. Production work tested those ideas against messy data, stakeholder constraints, adoption friction, and operational risk.

The result was a shift in how I think about the work. I no longer see the final product as a report or dashboard by itself. I see the product as a shared operating picture: a structure people can use to understand the current state, make decisions, and review what happened later.
