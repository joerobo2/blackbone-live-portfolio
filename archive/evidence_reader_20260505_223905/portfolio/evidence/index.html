Evidence

Sanitized technical proof layer.

This section explains the kinds of evidence that support the portfolio without exposing proprietary data, internal systems, or protected operational details.

Allowed evidence

  • Sanitized architecture summaries
  • Generalized database and workflow diagrams
  • Synthetic examples
  • Pseudocode
  • Public-safe process descriptions
  • Reflection on method and learning

Excluded evidence

  • Proprietary code
  • Internal URLs or system names
  • Production screenshots
  • Real operational data
  • Exact financial values
  • Customer, vendor, employee, or protected identifiers

Evidence model

The evidence layer is designed around a simple idea: a portfolio artifact should be strong enough to show skill, but safe enough to publish. The goal is not to reveal internal implementation. The goal is to demonstrate the reasoning pattern behind the implementation.

Each artifact should answer: what problem existed, what structure was introduced, what decision became easier, and what learning transferred into future work.

No proprietary data or internal artifacts.
